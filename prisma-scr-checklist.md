# PRISMA-ScR Checklist

Tricco AC, et al. (2018) PRISMA Extension for Scoping Reviews (PRISMA-ScR): Checklist and Explanation. *Ann Intern Med.* 169(7):467-473. doi:10.7326/M18-0850

| Item | Section | Checklist Item | Reported on Page |
|------|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | TITLE | Identify the report as a scoping review. | p. 1 |
| 2 | ABSTRACT | Provide a structured summary that includes (as applicable): background, objectives, eligibility criteria, sources of evidence, charting methods, results, and conclusions. | p. 1 |
| 3 | INTRODUCTION — Rationale | Describe the rationale for the review in the context of what is already known. | p. 2 |
| 4 | INTRODUCTION — Objectives | Provide an explicit statement of the questions and objectives being addressed. | p. 3 |
| 5 | METHODS — Protocol and Registration | Indicate whether a review protocol exists; state if and where it can be accessed. | p. 3 |
| 6 | METHODS — Eligibility Criteria | Specify characteristics of the sources of evidence used as eligibility criteria. | p. 3 |
| 7 | METHODS — Information Sources | Describe all information sources in the search. | p. 3 |
| 8 | METHODS — Search | Present the full electronic search strategy for at least 1 database. | p. 3 (Full search strings in supplementary materials) |
| 9 | METHODS — Selection of Sources of Evidence | State the process for selecting sources of evidence. | p. 3 |
| 10 | METHODS — Data Charting Process | Describe the methods of charting data from the included sources of evidence. | p. 4 |
| 11 | METHODS — Data Items | List and define all variables for which data were sought. | p. 4 |
| 12 | METHODS — Critical Appraisal (Optional) | If done, provide a rationale for conducting a critical appraisal. | Not conducted |
| 13 | METHODS — Summary Measures | N/A — Not applicable for scoping reviews. | N/A |
| 14 | METHODS — Synthesis of Results | Describe the methods of handling and summarizing the data that were charted. | p. 4 |
| 15 | METHODS — Risk of Bias Across Studies | N/A — Not applicable for scoping reviews. | N/A |
| 16 | METHODS — Additional Analyses | N/A — Not applicable for scoping reviews. | N/A |
| 17 | RESULTS — Selection of Sources of Evidence | Give numbers of sources of evidence screened, assessed for eligibility, and included in the review. | p. 4 (Figure 1) |
| 18 | RESULTS — Characteristics of Sources of Evidence | For each source of evidence, present characteristics for which data were charted. | p. 4 (Table 1) |
| 19 | RESULTS — Critical Appraisal Within Sources (Optional) | If done, present data on critical appraisal of included sources of evidence. | Not conducted |
| 20 | RESULTS — Results of Individual Sources of Evidence | For each included source of evidence, present the relevant data that were charted. | p. 4 (Tables 2–3) |
| 21 | RESULTS — Synthesis of Results | Summarize and/or present the charting results as they relate to the review questions. | p. 5 |
| 22 | RESULTS — Risk of Bias Across Studies | N/A — Not applicable for scoping reviews. | N/A |
| 23 | RESULTS — Additional Analyses | N/A — Not applicable for scoping reviews. | N/A |
| 24 | DISCUSSION — Summary of Evidence | Summarize the main results, link to the review questions and objectives. | __ (not found) |
| 25 | DISCUSSION — Limitations | Discuss the limitations of the scoping review process. | __ (not found) |
| 26 | DISCUSSION — Conclusions | Provide a general interpretation of the results with respect to the review questions. | __ (not found) |
| 27 | FUNDING | Describe sources of funding for the included sources of evidence, as well as sources of funding for the scoping review. | p. 8 |
